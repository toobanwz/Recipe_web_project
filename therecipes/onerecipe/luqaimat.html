Luqaimat

minutesPrep Time : 15 minutes |  Cook Time : 10 minutes minutes | Total Time : 25 minutes

A kind of Arabic sweet dumpling. Very famous dish that the Emaratis enjoy during Ramadan or other times.

Servings: 25 pieces  |  Calories : 353  |  Course: Breakfast  |  Cuisine: Indian  |  Diet: Gluten-Free, Vegan


Ingredients


1/2 cup All-purpose flour/Maida

1/4 cup + 2 tsp Luke-Warm Water (you may need less, depends on the flour you are using)

1/2 tbsp Corn flour

1/2 tbsp Instant Dry Yeast

1/4 tbsp Sugar

1/2 tsp Baking powder

1/2 tbsp Oil + Oil for Frying

3 tbsp Chocolate Syrup


Instructions


  1.   Firstly, add 1/4 cup of luke-warm water in a bowl. Add-in 1/2 tbsp yeast and 1/4 tbsp sugar to the water.

  2.   Mix well until sugar dissolves.

  3.   Now add in 1/2 cup maida flour, 1/2 tbsp corn flour, 1/2 tsp baking powder and start kneading.

  4.   Gradually add 2 tsp water and mix well. You may need less or more water, depending on the flour youâ€™re using.

  5.   Then add 1/2 tbsp oil. Mix well.

  6.   Cover and allow to rest for 15 to 20 minutes in a warm place till it has doubled in size.

  7.   After 20 minutes, the dough has doubled in size. Mix to release the air bubbles.

  8.   Now heat oil in a kadai or pan and drop the batter by spoonful. Alternatively you can drop the batter in a zip lock bag. Cut out the corner of the bag and push to get a small ball of the batter. Cut this batter using a scissor & drop this ball immediately in oil.

  9.   Roll the dropped balls with a slotted spoon in oil for even cooking. Fry till they are dark golden brown in color. Remove from oil.

  10.   Place them in a serving bowl and pour the chocolate syrup on top.
