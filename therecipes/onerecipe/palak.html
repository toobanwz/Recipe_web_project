Palak Aloo

minutesPrep Time : 10 minutes |  Cook Time : 20 minutes | Total Time : 30 minutes

An easy and simple dry curry recipe prepared with basic ingredients like spinach leaves and potato slices.

Servings: 4 |  Calories : 180  |  Course: Lunch/Dinner  |  Cuisine: North Indian  |  Diet: Gluten-Free, Vegan


Ingredients


¼ cup oil

1 tsp cumin

pinch hing

1 dried red chilli

5 cloves garlic, crushed

2 inch ginger, chopped

2 onion, finely chopped

3 potato, cubed

½ tsp turmeric

½ tsp salt

2 tbsp water

1 tsp chilli powder

1 tsp coriander powder

½ tsp cumin powder

½ tsp salt

3 tomato, chopped

1 bunch palak, chopped

½ tsp garam masala

2 tbsp kasuri methi, crushed

2 tbsp spring onion, chopped


Instructions


  1.   firstly, in a large kadai heat ¼ cup oil. saute 1 tsp cumin, pinch hing and 1 dried red chilli until they turn aromatic.

  2.   add 5 cloves garlic, 2 inch ginger and saute well.

  3.   further, add 2 onion and saute until the onion turns golden brown.

  4.   now add 3 potato, ½ tsp turmeric and ½ tsp salt.

  5.   stir fry for 2 minutes making sure the turmeric is well combined.

  6.   add 2 tbsp water, cover and simmer for 10 minutes or until the aloo is cooked well.

  7.   now add 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder and ½ tsp salt.

  8.   saute on low flame until the spices turn aromatic.

  9.   further, add 3 tomato and saute for 2 minutes.

  10.   also, add 1 bunch palak. do not mix the palak, as the leaves will not take much time to mix.

  11.   cover and simmer for 5 minutes or until palak and aloo are cooked completely.

  12.   add ½ tsp garam masala, 2 tbsp kasuri methi and 2 tbsp spring onion. mix well.

  13.   Now cut the dhokla into pieces.

  14.   To prepare the tempering, heat 2 tbsp oil. add 1 tsp mustard, 5 chilli, a few curry leaves, pinch hing, and splutter the tempering.

  15.   Further add ½ cup water, 2 tbsp sugar, and ¼ tsp salt.

  16.   Mix and boil for 2 minutes, making sure everything is well combined.

  17.   Pour the tempering over the dhokla and rest for 20 minutes so that the dhokla absorbs all the water.

  18.   Before serving, top with 2 tbsp coriander and 2 tbsp coconut.

  19.   Finally, enjoy nylon khaman dhokla recipe with green chutney.

perfect side dish for roti, chapati, and even for a dal rice combo.
